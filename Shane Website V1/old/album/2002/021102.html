Shane Zachary Cranor

18-19 Months

Shane stayed at the new Westin hotel in New York with Mom and Dad. Shane liked the window seat and the view. Shane also liked the elevator ("elbababer") -- that's a big word to say! New York is a very interesting, and Shane was so excited he did not want to go night night. When Shane got home the next day he did some puzzles with Dad.

Shane loves to play in the leaves. He helped Dad rake the leaves, and he learned to say rake and leaf blower. He is putting more words together into sentences now. When he can't find something he asks! For example, when he wants to know where the moon is he says "Where the moon go?" He also knows that the moon is in the sky, but sometimes the moon goes night night. He also asks to have things opened -- jars, yogurt containers, envelopes, straw wrappers, even bananas ("open mana")... sometimes he even says please. And he likes to close containers too ("lid on"). Shane really likes lights. He likes to say "light on." He also knows about red lights and green lights. If you ask him how he is he will tell you that he is "fine." Other new words he likes to say are clock, cracker, tofu, up ("up, up, up"), down ("I want doowwn"), fire truck, hippopotamus, help, lap, fabric, Elmo, Mickey, tea, piggy, and cow. Shane also likes to sing. He can take any word you suggest to him and repeat it over and over again in a song.

Shane went with Mom and Dad to Aunt Bonnie and Uncle Fred's house for Thanksgiving dinner. Shane didn't eat any turkey but he ate lots of crackers. The next day Shane kept saying "Uncle Fred!" This year Chanukah started the night after Thanksgiving. Shane likes to unwrap his presents. Lighting the candles and playing with dreidles is lots of fun. Shane says "dreidle spin" and "candle light." Shane also says "red light" and "green light" when he is in the car.

This week was very exciting. Shane went to mom and dad's office and played with some other kids whose parents brought them to work. Shane says "office" and "kids." The next day Shane went to the Big Apple Circus with mom and dad and Yvonne and her grandson. The circus was very exciting, and riding on the train and the subway to get there was fun too. The day after that we got an early snow storm and dad had to put the snow plow on the tractor to clear the driveway. But the snow did not delay the construction on our basement, which is still ahead of schedule.

Shane likes to play with his trucks and hide. He is saying more phrases and sentences: "Shane hiding," "drinking the water," "I want flakes in the green bowl," "I want to go go green car," "I want green light," "up mama," "up dada," "up bed," "down Shane," "boy chair" (in reference to a "big boy chair" as opposed to a high chair), and "no nooo apple" (or toast, or Os, or whatever it is he wants to make very clear that he doesn't want). This week Shane got to have lunch with Ann and Paul who were visiting from Chicago.

Shane likes to eat "bootie" in a cup. He is very excited about the basement construction, which is coming along nicely.

Shane went to visit Grandma Connie and Papa Morris for Christmas. Justin and Aunt Ginny and Uncle Jeff and Tyler the dog were there too. Shane got a new tricycle for Christmas. Justin and Shane tried out Grandma's new bath tub.

After Christmas, Shane went to visit Grandma Judy and Papa Mike. Auntie Amber and Uncle Jere and Robroy doggie and Marie the cat were there too. Shane likes Papa Mike's choo choos. Robroy doggie and the kitty cat made a big impression on him too. Shane also got to visit the National Zoo. He saw the camels and the elephants and the giraffes and the monkeys. When Shane got home he watched Dad try out his Christmas present -- a carpet cleaner.
