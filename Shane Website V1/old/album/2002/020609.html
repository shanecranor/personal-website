Shane Zachary Cranor

13-14 Months

Shane went to New York City with Mom and Dad. It was very exciting! Then when Shane got home Grandma Connie and Grandpa Morris stopped by on their way to the airport. Some of Shane's new words include: bread, cheese, door, keys, stick, dirt, and flower. Shane still likes to say "baby" especially when he eats YoBaby yogurt. He also says "bye bye" when he sees the telephone.

Shane likes to play with balls. He also likes to point at balls and talk about balls. Puddles and sticks and dirt are fun to play with too. Shane got a new hat with a chin strap to make it difficult to take off.

Shane took the train to Maryland with Mom and Dad. He had lots of fun with Grandma Judy and Grandpa Mike while Mom went to a conference. He went to their offices and he got to play with Grandpa Mike's trains. He also got to see Uncle Jeremy and Aunt Amber and Robroy the greyhound. After Mom's conference was over Shane and Mom and Dad went to visit Aunt Ginny and Uncle Jeff and cousin Justin. Justin likes balls too. Before going home, Shane visited lots of monuments in Washington, DC with Mom and Dad.

Shane went to some picnics with Mom and Dad. He got to play with Mom's boss's kids and also hit Dad's boss on the head. He also got to watch the Randolph fireworks, which were very cool (last year he slept through them). Shane learned how to say "Ollie" (the name of one of Dad's friends), socks, spoon, bursh, comb, potty, socks, tummy, eye, ear, bubbles, bottle, bath, bag, cat, monkey, and berry.

Mom and Dad bought a backpack that Shane can ride in. Shane gets a good view from up there. Shane likes to collect the dish towels in the kitchen. Sometimes he puts them in his cart and pushes them around -- some times he just waves them in the air. He also likes to pick up the milk and juice containers (and any other garbage he can get out of the recycle bin). He can also say milk and juice, as well as choo choo (as in train).
