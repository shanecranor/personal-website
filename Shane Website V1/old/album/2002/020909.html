Shane Zachary Cranor

16-17 Months

Shane likes to play with his toy doggies. Shane has learned how to use a spoon ("poon"). He likes to eat rice ("mai"), yo-baby ("baby"), and banana ("mana"). He also likes to say outside ("tide"), rain, wet, cookie, scone, down, blue, flakes, toast, basket, CD, cold, cat, monkey, bear, birdy, ducky, fishy, foggy, box, tounge ("gung"), teeth, and pretzle. Shane can also count to four.

Shane got dressed up in his spiffy clothes (courtesy of Auntie Amber) to attend his first wedding. Milind finally stopped dilly dallying around and tied the knot with Aparna! Zubin flew in from California for the big event. On the way home from the wedding Zubin and Shane went night night.

The leaves are starting to fall and Shane is very excited to see so many leaves on the ground. He is helping Mom and Dad clean them up, one leaf at a time. Shane likes to be helpful. Besides picking up leaves, he can put things in the garbage and "help" put away the clean laundry. Shane can say his first name now and count to 10. He can also say "E" as in Yvonne. He has lots of new words: phone, garbage, up, crayon, purple, green, red, plate, fork, juice, pumpkin, pasta, pizza, diaper, wipes, butt, peel (as in banana), flakes (as in corn flakes), clothes, tie (as in shoe), belt, pants, shirt, undies, bra, boat, cracker, bounce, paper, mail, pest, and OK ("kay"), and there you go. He knows that doggies say woof woof, cats say meow, duckies say quack quack, birdies say tweet tweet, cars say beep beep, fishies say blub blub, and monkeys say ee-eee. He know that E comes on the choo choo, and he recognizes the sound of the choo choo when he hears it. And he likes to sing E-I-E-I-O. Shane had lots of visitors over the past few weeks. It is getting too cold outside for sandles, so Shane got some new shoes.

Shane got a new winter coat. It is blue and orange -- like his pumpkin. Shane thinks pumpkins are pretty neat. He was very excited to watch Mom and Dad turn his pumpkin into a jack-o-lantern. Shane dressed up as a doggy for Halloween. He learned to say "tail," "candy," and "ghost." What do ghosts say? "Boo" of course!
